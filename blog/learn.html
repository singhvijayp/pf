learning cybersecurity slowly

most people rush through cybersecurity trying to collect tools, certifications and buzzwords as quickly as possible. i think the slower route might actually be better.

when i first started learning cybersecurity, i thought progress meant learning everything at once. linux, networking, burp suite, web vulnerabilities, reverse engineering, cryptography — all immediately.

eventually i realized the internet rewards speed, but real understanding usually comes from repetition. doing the same thing multiple times until it becomes intuitive.

now i spend more time understanding why something works instead of just memorizing steps from tutorials.

"slow learning compounds harder than rushed learning."

bug bounty hunting especially teaches patience. most of the work isn't glamorous — it's reading documentation, testing weird edge cases and staying curious longer than other people.

i also started appreciating minimal workflows more. fewer tools, cleaner setups, less distraction. calm software makes it easier to focus deeply.

right now i'm mainly focusing on:

  • web security fundamentals
  • understanding http deeply
  • javascript behavior
  • recon methodology
  • building things consistently

i still don't know where all of this leads exactly. but that's probably okay.
